Programming

I'm a self-learning programmer, looking for junior jobs, mostly in frontend.
In 2014, I've decided to start using macros (VBA) to better serve my colleagues' needs by simplifying and speeding up (HR and accounting related) administrative tasks significantly. I've reached a mid-level in about 2 years, reading documentations and experimenting on over-complicated, time-consuming and monotonic work processes.
It was some years ago, when I started to learn programming more intensively and also in a wider range.
First, I was about to gain general knowledge with Python - and PyGame, for the fun part. Mostly using Tkinter/ttk GUIs for my desktop apps ever since then, but also tried out Kivy/kv in Java. For databases, I use SQL, MySql (or JSON, on web), SQLite - and a bit of PHP, for contact, guestbooks and such. My Python is on a stable intermediate level. As for web development, my favourite is React, but I got to know Flask, too.
After several template projects for my CV/portfolio, my Javascript (Node.js) skills have improved a lot. Besides vanilla Javascript (simple 2D canvas animations), recently I started to turn towards this specific kind of web animations (3D scene) with ThreeJS.
I'm using (in order of preference) VS Code, PyCharm, Sublime Text, Notepad++ for coding and Git/GitHub for storing, handling, updating my code and stuff. Prettier and ESLint keep my code clean, easy-to-read.

Freetime Activities / Hobbies

Poems

I've been writing poems since I was 15 years old. They function as quasi diary for me - as a way to air out my feelings and thoughts from time to time. They are usually publicated on poetry related websites, but some of them have showed up in a dozen of antologies. Among all, my own published volume of poetry I'm the most proud of.

Reading

Reading has always been important for me, be it novels, poems, comics or magazines. I love sci-fi, fantasy, thriller, horror, historical or any kind of interesting stories. Besides being food for thoughts and imagination, reading is a good relaxation practice and helps improving vocabulary, too. Anyway, it's fun.

Sci-fi, astronomy

As were mentioned above in connection with books and reading: I especially fond of astronomy and our vast universe. Therefore, it goes without saying that scientific educational and documentary films, plus all thought-provoking, sci-fi genre movies are close to my heart, to say the least.

Photography

I find great pleasure in nature/landscape photography and have made some success in photo contests (calendars), too.

Hiking, cycling

Nature is important to me, hiking fills me with joy. In nature there's always something beautiful to see, some interesting animal species in their habitats to observe - and being said above, to take a photo of. As for pets, I prefer dogs.

Music

Not being so genre-specific, I listen to various music genres. But, of course, there are times when one becomes fan of some specific genres. Nowadays my absolute favourites are the retro synthwave tunes, along with melodic sorts of techno, minimal, dupstep, etc.
